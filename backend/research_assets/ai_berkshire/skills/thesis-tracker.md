# Thesis Tracker — Investment Thesis Verification

## Skill Description
Systematically review whether the original investment thesis for a specific holding still holds, identify which assumptions have been validated or falsified, and determine whether the position warrants continued ownership.

## Core Principle
"The most dangerous words in investing are: the thesis hasn't changed." — Revisit your thesis rigorously, not to confirm it, but to falsify it.

## Tracking Framework

### Phase 1 — Reconstruct the Original Thesis
Answer these in ≤ 200 words:
- What was the core insight that justified purchase?
- What did the market misunderstand or undervalue at the time of purchase?
- What was the expected time horizon for the thesis to play out?
- What price / valuation signaled the original entry?

### Phase 2 — Key Assumption Audit

### Phase 3 — New Information Assessment
Since the original purchase:
- What positive developments support the thesis?
- What negative developments challenge the thesis?
- What unexpected risks have emerged?
- Have any competitors made significant moves that affect the moat assessment?

### Phase 4 — Valuation Update
- What is the current intrinsic value estimate (updated with current data)?
- How has the price/value relationship changed since purchase?
- At current price, what is the expected return over the original time horizon?
- If you did not already own this stock, would you buy it today at the current price? Why or why not?

### Phase 5 — Thesis Verdict and Action

Overall thesis status:
- 🟢 **Intact**: Core thesis still valid, continue holding
- 🟡 **Under Review**: Key assumption uncertain, monitor closely with defined triggers
- 🔴 **Broken**: Thesis has been falsified, exit discipline required

Complete all 5 phases. The verdict must be explicit — "still watching" is not a verdict.
