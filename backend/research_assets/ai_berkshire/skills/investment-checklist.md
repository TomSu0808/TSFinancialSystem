# Investment Checklist — Pre-Buy Verification

## Skill Description
Systematically answer every item on this checklist before committing capital. Any "No" or "Uncertain" requires explicit written justification — not hand-waving.

## Philosophy
Buffett: "I don't look to jump over 7-foot bars; I look around for 1-foot bars that I can step over."
Munger: "Invert, always invert — what could go wrong?"

## Checklist

### A. Business Understanding
- [ ] Can I explain how this company earns money in 2 sentences a non-expert would understand?
- [ ] Do I understand the main customer group and the reason they pay?
- [ ] Can I describe the unit economics at maturity?
- [ ] Is the business model getting stronger, staying the same, or weakening?
- [ ] What are the 3 biggest structural tailwinds for this industry over the next 10 years?

### B. Competitive Moat
- [ ] Can I identify at least one clear, specific source of competitive advantage?
- [ ] Has ROIC been consistently > 12% over the past 5 years?
- [ ] Is market share stable or growing (not declining)?
- [ ] Would a well-funded competitor find it difficult or uneconomic to replicate this business?
- [ ] Is the moat likely to be stronger or weaker in 10 years?

### C. Management Quality
- [ ] Does management have a track record of disciplined capital allocation?
- [ ] Is insider ownership meaningful (> 5%) without excessive selling signals?
- [ ] Has management delivered on major multi-year commitments?
- [ ] Are CEO compensation and shareholder returns meaningfully linked?
- [ ] Is the CFO and audit committee genuinely independent?

### D. Financial Health
- [ ] Has free cash flow been positive for at least 3 of the last 5 years?
- [ ] Is net debt / EBITDA < 3×?
- [ ] Is operating cash flow ≥ 80% of reported net income (quality check)?
- [ ] Are there any off-balance-sheet liabilities or pending litigation that could be material?
- [ ] Is the most recent audit opinion clean (no qualifications)?

### E. Valuation Sanity
- [ ] Have I calculated intrinsic value using at least 2 independent methods?
- [ ] Does the current price offer a margin of safety ≥ 20% in the base case?
- [ ] Have I stress-tested the bear case: what is the downside if my 2 biggest assumptions are wrong?
- [ ] Is the current valuation at a discount to its own 5-year median and peer median?
- [ ] Can I articulate my exit thesis (what changes to make me sell)?

### F. Risk Acknowledgement
- [ ] Have I identified the 3 greatest downside risks?
- [ ] Am I comfortable owning this position if the stock drops 30% on no fundamental change?
- [ ] Is the position size appropriate given the uncertainty level?
- [ ] Is there a concentrated client, supplier, or regulatory dependency that could impair the thesis?

## Output Format
For each section:
- State pass / fail / uncertain for each item
- For any "fail" or "uncertain": provide 1–2 sentence explanation
- Section summary: Proceed / Flag / Reject with rationale
- Overall verdict: **Green Light / Yellow Flag / Red Stop**
