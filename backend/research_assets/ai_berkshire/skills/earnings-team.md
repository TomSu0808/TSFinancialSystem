# Earnings Team — Post-Earnings Committee Discussion

## Skill Description
Simulate a structured post-earnings investment team discussion where analysts from different functional areas dissect the earnings report and reach a collective action decision.

## Team Roles

**The Fundamental Analyst** — focuses on revenue quality, margins, and unit economics
**The Accounting Skeptic** — looks for earnings manipulation, hidden liabilities, quality issues
**The Management Watcher** — evaluates management credibility, guidance, and communication
**The Portfolio Manager** — synthesizes all inputs into a position-sizing decision

## Discussion Structure

### Opening: Fundamental Analyst's Summary (5 minutes)
Present the 5 most important data points from the report:
1. Revenue growth (organic, constant currency)
2. Margin performance vs. expectations
3. Cash flow conversion quality
4. Guidance change (raised / lowered / withdrawn)
5. Biggest positive surprise and biggest negative surprise

### Cross-Examination: Accounting Skeptic's Questions
The Skeptic raises 3–5 specific concerns about the quality of the numbers:
- "Walk me through why operating cash flow lagged net income by X%..."
- "This is the 4th consecutive quarter with 'restructuring charges' — when does this normalize?"
- "Accounts receivable grew 3× faster than revenue — management's explanation?"

The Fundamental Analyst must respond with data, not narrative.

### Management Assessment: Management Watcher
- Compare prior quarter's guidance to this quarter's actual result — % accuracy
- Evaluate the CEO's / CFO's tone on the earnings call: specific and accountable vs. vague and deflecting
- Note any significant changes in disclosure practices (new metrics introduced, old metrics dropped)
- Rate management credibility this quarter: **Rising / Stable / Declining**

### Synthesis: Portfolio Manager's Decision
After hearing all inputs, the Portfolio Manager must decide:

**Action**: Add / Hold / Reduce / Exit  
**Conviction change**: Increased / Unchanged / Decreased  
**New position size target** (if changing): X% of portfolio  
**Key condition to revisit**: [specific metric or event]

## Rules
- All factual claims must reference specific line items from the earnings report
- Disagreements between team members must be stated explicitly
- The final PM decision must reconcile with the Skeptic's concerns, not ignore them
- No hedging language like "it's complicated" — force a clear verdict
