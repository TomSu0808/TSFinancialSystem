# DYP Ask — Duan Yongping Style Questions

## Skill Description
Generate and answer the key questions Duan Yongping (段永平) would ask about a company. DYP is known for cutting through complexity to the essential truth with deceptively simple but penetrating questions.

## The DYP Framework

Duan Yongping's core investing principles:
- **Stop Doing List (不为清单)**: Define what you will NEVER do before defining what you will do
- **Right Things Right (做对的事，把事做对)**: Business model integrity over short-term metrics
- **Long-term ownership mindset**: Would I be comfortable owning this for 20 years?
- **Business owner perspective**: Am I buying a business or trading a stock?
- **Extreme simplicity**: If I can't explain why I own it in one sentence, I don't understand it

## DYP Questions — Answer Each Honestly

### The Essential Question
**"If I had to summarize why this company will still be doing well in 20 years in one sentence, what would I say?"**
*(If you can't answer this, stop here)*

### Business Integrity Questions
1. "Does this company do things that make me uncomfortable, even if they're legal and profitable?" (DYP's "stop doing" filter)
2. "Would this company's customers and employees speak well of it when I'm not in the room?"
3. "Is the CEO obsessing over the right things (product quality, customer value) or the wrong things (quarterly EPS, stock price)?"

### Moat Reality Check
4. "If this company didn't exist tomorrow, would customers be genuinely upset — or would they easily switch to an alternative?"
5. "Has this company ever had to cut its price to keep customers? What does that tell you?"
6. "What would it take for a startup with unlimited capital to destroy this business in 10 years?"

### Capital Allocation Questions
7. "When this company had excess cash in the last 5 years, what did they do with it? Was that the right choice?"
8. "Does the CEO own a meaningful amount of stock in this company? Did they buy it with their own money or did they get it from options?"

### Long-Term Durability
9. "What is the company's 'stop doing list' — things they refuse to do even under pressure for short-term results?"
10. "In 10 years, will this company's core advantage be stronger or weaker than today? What's the most honest answer?"

### The Valuation Question (DYP Style)
11. "If this company were private and not tradeable for 10 years, would I still want to own it at this price? What cash would I realistically receive over that period?"
12. "What is my worst realistic scenario, and can I live with it financially and emotionally?"

## Output Format
For each of the 12 questions:
- State the question
- Provide an honest, direct answer based on the available evidence
- Rate the answer: **Positive / Neutral / Negative** signal for the investment

Final DYP Verdict:
- **All signals positive**: Strong buy candidate
- **Mostly positive, minor concerns**: Watchlist with conditions
- **Mixed signals**: Pass — only invest in what you understand completely
- **Any fundamental negative**: Stop here, do not invest
