# Industry Research — Structural Industry Analysis

## Skill Description
Analyze the competitive structure, supply/demand dynamics, and long-term attractiveness of an industry as a hunting ground for high-quality investments.

## Analytical Foundation
Apply Porter's Five Forces + Buffett's "wonderful business in a wonderful industry" filter. The goal is to identify whether this industry can sustain above-average returns over a full market cycle.

## Research Framework

### Force 1 — Competitive Rivalry
- Concentration ratio (CR4, CR8) and trend (consolidating vs. fragmenting)
- Nature of competition: price-based or value/quality-based?
- Industry growth rate: high growth attracts entrants; slow growth intensifies rivalry
- Exit barriers: how easily can weak players exit? High exit barriers worsen rivalry

### Force 2 — Threat of New Entrants
- Capital requirements and minimum efficient scale
- Regulatory / licensing barriers
- Incumbent advantages: patents, brand, distribution, data, network effects
- Recent entry history: have new entrants gained meaningful share in the last 5 years?

### Force 3 — Supplier Power
- Concentration of key input suppliers
- Availability of substitutes for key inputs
- Switching costs for the industry when changing suppliers
- Any vertical integration trend by suppliers into the industry?

### Force 4 — Buyer Power
- Customer concentration: does any single buyer have pricing leverage?
- Price sensitivity: are buyers purely price-driven or do they value quality / relationships?
- Backward integration threat: can major customers produce in-house?
- Switching costs for customers within this industry

### Force 5 — Substitution Threat
- Are there alternative products / services that could replace the industry's core offering?
- Rate of technology change threatening incumbents
- Cost/performance trajectory of substitutes

### Industry Attractiveness Score
Rate each force: **Favorable / Neutral / Unfavorable** for incumbent profitability.
Overall industry attractiveness: **High / Medium / Low**
Key metric: 10-year median ROIC for industry participants vs. industry WACC estimate.

### Long-Term Structural Trends
- Demand drivers over 10 years (demographics, regulation, technology)
- Supply-side evolution (consolidation, capex cycles, raw material trends)
- Disruption risk assessment: probability × severity × time horizon

## Output Format
Cover all 5 forces with specific data points. Provide the overall industry verdict with supporting evidence. Name the 3 most attractive positions within this industry (by competitive positioning, not current valuation).
