# Earnings Review — Deep Earnings Analysis

## Skill Description
Conduct a rigorous analysis of the most recent earnings report, separating signal from noise, evaluating earnings quality, and updating the investment thesis.

## Analyst Mindset
Reported earnings are management's version of events. Your job is to find the economic truth underneath the accounting. Focus on cash generation, not EPS.

## Analysis Framework

### Section 1 — Revenue Quality
- Total revenue vs. prior quarter / prior year (both reported and constant-currency)
- Revenue breakdown by segment: which segments grew, which declined?
- **Organic vs. acquired growth**: strip out acquisitions to see true organic rate
- **Deferred revenue trend**: is the future revenue pipeline growing or shrinking?
- **Customer concentration**: any disclosure of top-customer revenue dependency changes?

### Section 2 — Profit Quality
- Gross margin: actual vs. prior periods vs. consensus estimate
- Operating margin: adjusted vs. GAAP — what adjustments are being excluded and why?
- Identify one-time items: are "one-time" charges recurring in practice?
- EBITDA vs. actual FCF: a wide gap suggests high capex or working capital consumption

### Section 3 — Cash Flow Health
The most important number: **FCF conversion = Operating Cash Flow / Net Income**
- If conversion < 70%: investigate why (accruals, capex, working capital trap)
- Capex trend: maintenance vs. growth capex split (if disclosed)
- Working capital changes: are receivables and inventory growing faster than revenue? (Red flag)
- Share count change: dilution from stock comp vs. buyback discipline

### Section 4 — Management Guidance Credibility
- Prior quarter guidance vs. actual outcome — did they beat, meet, or miss?
- New guidance for next quarter / full year — is it realistic or management-sandbagging?
- Tone of earnings call: confident specifics vs. vague qualitative deflection
- Any change in key performance metrics disclosed (a metric that disappears is a red flag)

### Section 5 — Thesis Update
Based on this earnings report:
- Which assumptions in the original investment thesis were confirmed?
- Which were challenged?
- Has the long-term intrinsic value estimate changed? By how much and why?
- Updated recommendation: **Thesis intact / Watch closely / Re-evaluate**

## Output Format
Complete all 5 sections with specific numbers cited from the earnings report. Avoid generic commentary — every observation must be grounded in a specific data point with its source (earnings release, 10-Q, earnings call transcript).
